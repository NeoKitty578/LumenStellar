What is stellar phyisics?

Stellar physics is the study of stars. How they form, produce energy, evolve, and die. It's a core branch of Astrophysics. It combines thermodynamics, nuclear phyics and the gravity.

Stage 1:The gravitational collapse

So gravity pulls gas inwards. Then as the cloud contracts, this is what happens:

  • Density increases
  • Temperature rises
  • Pressure builds

Stage 2:Protor star

So this part is where the star is just "loading", it isnt a star just yet but it's in formation. The core becomes dense.

Stage 3:Fusion ignition

Now Kelvin is the International System of Units (SI) base unit for thermodynamic temperature, basically a way to measure temperature in space. When the core 10 million Kelvin, hydrogen fusion starts, then baaaam!!!! The protostar becomes a real star.

There's certain things that need to keep the star stable. Its gravity, hydrostatic equilibrium and radiatiion pressure. Inside the star it has layers just like our star the Sun.

Now how did scientists find out about exoplanets?

Exoplanets can really be detected because they're extremely bright, so scientists use methods.

  • Transit method

    • Most famous method. When a planet passes in front of the star becomes slightly dimmer. A tiny bit of brightness means possible planet.

  • Radial velocity method

    • A planet's gravity slightly pulls on its star, then it wobbles. Then scientist detect the "wobbling" with Doppler effect. That helps determine the planet mass and it's orbit

  • Direct imaging

    • This one is quite rare. This method can be used for giant planet because their easier to separate visually.

  • Gravitational microlensing

    • This method uses gravity as a lens. According to OpenAI a foreground star bends light from a background star. If a planet is present, a special brightness appears
